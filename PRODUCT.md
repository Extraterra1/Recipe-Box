# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Recipe Box is a personal cookbook for one user. It is used primarily on an iPhone to find a saved recipe quickly, open it while cooking, and maintain a dependable private archive. The collection includes recipes with and without photography, so recognition cannot depend on imagery.

## Product Purpose

Recipe Box turns an existing recipe archive into a focused personal cooking tool with search, offline reading, editing, import, and export. The home screen should make the full collection immediately browsable: identity first, search second, recipes immediately after. Success means a recipe can be found and opened in seconds without navigating through categories, dashboards, or decorative content.

## Brand Personality

Modern, composed, and practical. The product should have the confidence and familiarity of a well-made native iOS utility. Paprika is a workflow reference for directness and usefulness, not a visual reference. The interface should feel current without chasing trends or turning a personal cookbook into a lifestyle brand.

## Anti-references

Do not reproduce Paprika's dated Android styling, dense red toolbars, legacy controls, or desktop-derived layouts. Avoid generic AI-generated design signals: gradients, glass effects, excessive pills, oversized radii, floating badges, eyebrow labels, randomly expressive typography, decorative cards, cute kitchen illustrations, verbose copy, and ornamental color. Do not make recipe photography the organizing principle because many recipes have no image.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the recipe name. Titles are the most dependable recognition signal across the whole archive.
2. Make the collection immediate. Logo, name, search, and recipes form the home screen, with no promotional or dashboard layer between them.
3. Keep missing imagery intentional. Every recipe occupies the same stable structure; a quiet icon placeholder replaces absent photography without pretending to be content.
4. Earn an iOS-native feel. Use familiar hierarchy, controls, transitions, and touch behavior so the interface disappears into the task.
5. Modernize through precision. Spacing, type, alignment, and state design provide character; decoration does not.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

There is no formal accessibility conformance target. Preserve baseline iOS usability through legible text, clear contrast, predictable controls, and comfortable touch targets. Do not add accessibility-themed visual treatment or complexity that competes with the personal cooking workflow.
