# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Recipe Box is for a household that wants one shared, dependable cookbook across phones and computers. Users may be cooking with messy hands, checking a step mid-recipe, adding a family favorite, or searching for something familiar before shopping. The primary context is practical and domestic: quick lookup, confident reading, easy editing, and offline access when the kitchen connection is unreliable.

## Product Purpose

Recipe Box turns an existing Paprika recipe archive into a small household PWA with cloud sync, offline reads, and shared editing. Its job is to make saved recipes easy to find, read, preserve, and update without the weight of a public recipe site or meal-planning suite. Success means the household trusts it as the canonical cookbook: searchable, installable, comfortable on an iPhone, and resilient enough to use while actually cooking.

## Brand Personality

Light, capable, and homey. The product should feel warm and playful without becoming childish, more like a thoughtfully organized kitchen drawer than a productivity dashboard. It can have gentle personality in empty states, tags, and sync messaging, but the tone should stay direct when users are cooking or editing.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-page fluff, recipe-blog clutter, dark productivity dashboards, sterile enterprise admin UI, and generic SaaS polish. Do not make the app feel like a content platform, social feed, or monetized recipe site. Avoid visual choices that compete with recipe readability, including heavy decoration, novelty typography, low-contrast pastel text, and interactions that require precision when a simple tap should do.

## Design Principles

1. Prioritize cooking context: recipe pages should be readable at a glance, resilient to interruption, and comfortable on a phone propped up in a kitchen.
2. Make sharing feel household-native: membership, sync, invites, and ownership should feel calm and clear, not like managing enterprise permissions.
3. Preserve the archive: imported recipes are the heart of the product, so search, filtering, editing, and export should reinforce trust in the collection.
4. Keep the interface light but useful: playful accents are welcome when they clarify state or add warmth, but utility comes first.
5. Favor continuity over novelty: offline reads, sync status, and backups should make users feel that recipes will still be there when needed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for contrast, keyboard navigation, visible focus states, and form labeling. Use large touch targets suitable for phone use in a kitchen, avoid relying on color alone for favorites, sync, or validation states, and support reduced motion for any non-essential animation. Keep recipe text scalable and avoid dense layouts that become fragile on small screens.
